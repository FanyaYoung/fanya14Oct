LIFE ALERT

A Case Study

THE CHALLENGE

The Challenge: To modernize Life Alert and regain its target marketshare or the next generation of users.


USER RESEARCH

Seniors and their caregivers were the main demographic for survyes and interviews. Questions ranged from their beliefs about healthcare, fitness, and preferences for a mobile app design.



Creating Personas

Most customers are senior citizens. Therefore, great care went into developing the primary persona. Betty, the primary persona, reflects the ideaology, pragmatisim, and frugality of a 82 year-old woman born during the Great Depression.

THE "RE-DESIGN" PROCESS


    After creating personas, more design processes were utilized. . .

  • Mapping the Features

    Features are prioritized by whether it's essential to the apps ability to function and the cost of integrating the feature in the app.

  • An Affinity Map

    Ideas were jotted down on Post-Its. Notes with similar themes were grouped together.

  • User Journey Map 1

    User's had negative experiences with Life Alert after an initial incident resulting in emergency services. The solution: introduce Life Alert before the initial incident, before an ER visit. Life Alert would be preventative and encourage teens, adults, and seniors to maintain their health.

  • User Journey Map 2

    By providing metrics for fitness, health, and emergency services, Life Alert's new user experience is a positive one.
